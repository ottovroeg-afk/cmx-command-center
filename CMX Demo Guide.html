CMX Intelligence Engine — Demo Guide

Step-by-step walkthrough for demonstrating all assets to senior stakeholders
akt

What You're Demonstrating

A connected automation system that replaces 3-5 days of manual workshop preparation with a 30-minute intelligence pipeline. Stakeholder input is collected via structured questionnaires, analyzed by an AI-powered engine, and converted into production-ready workshop artifacts — all pre-populated with real data.

The demo uses Nextera Energy (GMTP program) as a live scenario with 12 pre-filled respondents across Executive, Operational, Technical, and Regional roles.

Total demo time:  ~12 minutes
Assets covered:  10 files
Client scenario:  Nextera Energy
No setup needed:  All data pre-loaded
Part A — Context Setting
1
Open the Senior Team Introduction Deck

Part B — The Collection Layer
2
Show the WS1 Pre-Workshop Questionnaire
2 min
CMX Pre-Workshop Questionnaire.html

This is the WS1 Governance questionnaire with 12 pre-filled Nextera respondents, 33 questions across 8 decision blocks.

  • Open the HTML file in any browser — works fully offline
  • Point out the 12 respondent cards at the top — each shows name, role, department
  • Click on any respondent card to open their full response in a modal
  • Scroll through the questions — highlight how they're grouped by governance decision
  • Show the role-adaptive branching (Executive sees governance questions, Technical sees data questions)
  • Click the "Export CSV for Intelligence Engine" button
  • Show the CSV preview that appears — this is what feeds the engine
Key message: "This replaces the email chain. Instead of chasing 12 people for input, they get a structured survey. In Qualtrics, this would use branch logic to show role-specific questions. The CSV export is what we feed into the engine."

CMX WS2/WS3/WS4 Pre-Workshop Questionnaires + DW1/DW2 Session Outputs.html

4

5

30 sec
Qualtrics Test Export - CMX WS1.csv

6
Run the Intelligence Analysis
2 min

This is the showstopper. Click "Run Analysis" and walk through what the engine produces.

  • Click "Run Analysis" — the intelligence panel appears
  • Walk through the readiness radar — shows 5 dimensions scored automatically
  • Show the decision cards — each of the 8 governance decisions gets:
       • Consensus score (role-weighted)
       • ADKAR stage mapping
       • Risk signals
       • Consultant-grade summary text
  • Highlight role divergence — where Executive and Regional disagree
  • Show the risk dashboard — 6 CMX-specific risk categories
  • Point out facilitator playbooks — per-decision warm-ups and watch-outs
Key message: "This analysis reads like a senior consultant wrote it. Role-weighted consensus, ADKAR mapping, divergence detection — all computed in seconds from raw survey data. This is the intelligence that used to take days to compile."
7
Generate All Artifacts
1 min

Click "Generate All" and download the three outputs.

  • Click "Generate All" — three download buttons appear

  • Download the XLSX Open Points Tracker — show 5 sheets: Open Points, Decision Log, Risk Register, Stakeholder Map, Governance Cadence
  • Download the HTML Facilitator Briefing — show the print-ready briefing pack with per-decision intelligence
Key message: "Three production-ready artifacts in one click. The deck isn't a template — it's filled with real data. The tracker isn't blank — it's seeded with open items from the survey. The briefing pack gives the facilitator everything they need for Day 1."

Demo Tips

Before the demo: Open all HTML files in separate browser tabs so you can switch quickly. Have the PPTX open in presentation mode.

Internet not required: Everything runs locally in the browser. No server, no API calls, no cloud dependencies. You can demo on a plane.

If something goes wrong: The "Load Demo Data" button in the Intelligence Engine is your safety net — it pre-populates everything instantly without needing the CSV import.

For maximum impact: Pause after the intelligence analysis (Step 6) and ask: "How long would this take a consultant to produce manually?" Let the audience answer before you show the one-click generation.
