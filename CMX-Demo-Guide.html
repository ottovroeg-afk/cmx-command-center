The Basics

What is CMX?

CMX (Change Management Experience) is a browser-based command center that helps AKT consultants run stakeholder workshops more efficiently. It takes pre-workshop survey data from Qualtrics, analyses it automatically, and produces ready-to-use workshop materials — all in one place.

Think of it as the "mission control" for change management programs. Instead of spending days manually analysing survey responses and building decks, CMX does it in minutes.
📈
Real-Time Dashboard
Live view of stakeholder readiness, consensus levels, risk signals, and open points across the entire program.
🧠
Intelligence Engine
4-step wizard that imports Qualtrics data, analyses stakeholder positions, and identifies where people agree — and where they don't.
📚
Artifact Generation
Auto-generates branded workshop decks (PPTX), open-point trackers (XLSX), and facilitator briefings (HTML) — all pre-populated with data.

🎯 Dashboard

The home screen. Shows the big numbers at a glance: overall readiness (68%), average consensus (72%), risk signals (4), open points (9), and respondent count (12). Also shows a role-distribution breakdown (Executive, Operational, Technical, Regional) and quick-action buttons to jump to other features.

🚀 Pipeline

Shows all 6 stages of the CMX Foundations Program — 4 workshops (WS1–WS4) and 2 decision workshops (DW1–DW2). Each stage shows its status: "Workshop Delivered", "Artifacts Generated", or "Analysis Complete". This is where you track what's done and what's next.

🧠 Intelligence

The analytical heart of CMX. Contains two things: the 4-step Intelligence Engine wizard (Configure → Import Data → Intelligence → Generate) and the always-on dashboard intelligence showing decision analysis, risk signals, and a full stakeholder readiness table. This is where raw survey data becomes actionable insight.

📊 Deck Engine

Generates professional AKT-branded PowerPoint decks with one click. Currently supports WS1 (Governance & Decision Framework) — a 7-slide deck with stakeholder overview, risk dashboard, consensus analysis, and decision deep-dives. WS2 is coming next.

☑ Progress

Interactive checklists for all 6 program stages. Track completion items like "Pre-survey completed", "Intelligence analysed", "Facilitator briefing prepared". Click to check off items as they're done.

📦 Assets

Central document library. Links to the Intelligence Engine, WS1 Questionnaire (33 questions), Demo Guide, session outputs for DW1 and DW2, the Program Hub, PPT Engine architecture docs, and downloadable files (PPTX decks, XLSX specs, CSV test data).

📅 Timeline

Visual program timeline in 3 phases: Phase 1 (Governance & Design), Phase 2 (Platform & Architecture), Phase 3 (Finalization & Go-Live). Shows sequencing of workshops, decision workshops, and key milestones.

How to Demo It

Recommended Demo Flow (10 min)

Follow these 5 steps when presenting CMX to leadership. The demo uses built-in Nextera Energy sample data, so no setup is needed.

1

Start on the Dashboard

Open CMX and talk through the headline numbers: 12 stakeholders surveyed, 68% readiness, 72% consensus, 4 risk signals. Point out that this used to take days to compile manually — now it's instant.

2

Show the Intelligence Engine

Click the "Intelligence Engine" quick action. Walk through the 4 steps: Configure (enter client name), Import Data (click "Load Nextera Demo Data"), view the Intelligence analysis (consensus bars, role divergence), then show the Generate panel where you can create all 3 artifacts in one click.

3

Click "Generate All 3 Artifacts"

This is the wow moment. One click produces a branded PPTX workshop deck, an XLSX open-points tracker, and an HTML facilitator briefing — all pre-filled with actual stakeholder intelligence. Open the PPTX to show the quality.

4

Walk Through the Stakeholder Table

Scroll down to the Stakeholder Readiness & Watch List. Show how each person's readiness score, primary concern, and risk flag are surfaced automatically. Point out the "Watch" flags on lower-readiness stakeholders.

5

Show Pipeline + Progress

Quick flip to the Pipeline tab to show the 6-stage program overview, then Progress to show how checklist items track completion. End with: "This is one program. The platform is designed to scale across all clients."

Capabilities

What CMX Can Do Today

  • Import Qualtrics CSV exports and auto-analyse stakeholder responses across all role types (Executive, Operational, Technical, Regional)
  • Calculate readiness scores, consensus percentages, and role-based metrics automatically
  • Identify risk signals (capacity gaps, alignment divergence, adoption blockers, data quality issues)
  • Surface decision-level analysis — e.g. "50% prefer Hybrid governance, but Exec and Regional roles diverge significantly"
  • Generate AKT-branded PPTX workshop decks (7 slides, pre-populated with client data) in seconds
  • Generate XLSX open-points trackers with owners, priorities, and categories pulled from survey responses
  • Generate HTML facilitator briefings with per-decision intelligence, consensus bars, and recommended positions
  • Track program progress across 6 stages (4 workshops + 2 decision workshops) with interactive checklists
  • Run entirely in the browser — no server, no login, no data leaves the device (privacy by design)
  • Work offline once loaded — perfect for client sites with restricted network access
  • Support demo mode with built-in Nextera Energy sample data (12 stakeholders) for instant walkthroughs
Current Limitations

What CMX Cannot Do (Yet)

  • Connect directly to Qualtrics API — data must be manually exported as CSV and imported into CMX
  • Support real-time multi-user collaboration — it's a single-user tool, not a shared workspace
  • Store data between sessions — refreshing the page resets everything (no database or cloud storage)
  • Generate decks for WS2, WS3, or WS4 — only WS1 (Governance & Decision Framework) is built so far
  • Use AI/LLM for natural-language analysis — all analysis is rule-based (thresholds and formulas, not AI)
  • Handle non-English survey responses or multi-language analysis
  • Produce editable, design-polished PPTX — the generated slides are functional but use basic programmatic styling
  • Scale to multiple simultaneous programs or clients within a single instance
  • Send notifications, reminders, or integrate with email/calendar systems
  • Track historical trends or compare across multiple survey waves

Where It's Going

Future Potential

CMX is a working prototype today. Here's the realistic roadmap for where it could go:

Near-Term (Next 3 months)
WS2–WS4 + DW2 Deck Generation
Extend the Deck Engine to auto-generate branded slides for all remaining workshop types: Stakeholder Mapping, Survey Design, Platform Architecture, and Dashboard Decisions.
Near-Term (Next 3 months)
Persistent Storage
Save program state to local storage or file export/import so consultants don't lose their work when the browser closes. Enable session continuity across days.
Medium-Term (3–6 months)
Direct Qualtrics Integration
Connect to the Qualtrics API to pull survey responses automatically — no more manual CSV exports. Real-time data refresh as new responses arrive.
Medium-Term (3–6 months)
AI-Powered Narrative Intelligence
Use LLMs (like Claude) to analyse open-text responses, generate executive summaries, and produce natural-language insights — "Here's what your stakeholders are really telling you."
Medium-Term (3–6 months)
Multi-Program Portfolio View
Manage multiple client programs from one dashboard. Compare readiness scores, share templates, and spot patterns across engagements.
Medium-Term (3–6 months)
Wave Comparison & Trend Tracking
Track how stakeholder readiness and consensus evolve over time. Compare pre-workshop and post-workshop survey waves side by side.
Longer-Term (6–12 months)
Client-Facing Portal
A read-only, branded version of the dashboard that clients can access directly. Live program transparency without giving access to the full consultant toolset.
Longer-Term (6–12 months)
Predictive Risk Engine
Move from reactive risk identification to predictive modelling. Flag programs likely to stall based on patterns from previous engagements.
Talking Points

Key Messages for Leadership

Speed

What used to take 2–3 days of manual analysis and deck-building now takes under 10 minutes. The Intelligence Engine processes 12+ stakeholder responses and generates 3 presentation-ready artifacts in one click.

Quality & Consistency

Every workshop gets the same rigorous analysis. No more varying quality based on which consultant is running the engagement. CMX enforces a standard methodology while remaining flexible to each client's context.

Privacy by Design

Everything runs in the browser. No servers, no cloud, no data transmission. Client data never leaves their device. This makes CMX deployable in even the most security-conscious enterprise environments.

Scalable IP

CMX turns AKT's consulting methodology into reusable, scalable software. As the tool matures, it becomes a competitive differentiator — clients get better outcomes, faster, with built-in AKT intelligence.
