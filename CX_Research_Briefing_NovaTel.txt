1. BACKGROUND AND CONTEXT

NovaTel Telecommunications Group is a mid-size European telecommunications provider with 4.2 million subscribers, generating EUR 2.14 billion in annual revenue. The company operates across consumer, business, and premium segments, offering mobile, broadband, and convergent services.

NovaTel has operated a Voice of Customer programme since 2019, using Qualtrics as the primary research platform. The programme includes:
- Quarterly relationship surveys (NPS, CSAT, attribute satisfaction)
- Post-interaction transactional surveys (contact centre, store, digital)
- Annual brand tracking study
- TextiQ-powered verbatim analysis
- StatsIQ key driver modelling

2. CURRENT SITUATION

NPS has declined from 32 (Q1 2025) to 24 (Q4 2025), representing the most significant decline in the programme's history. The decline is broad-based but most acute in digital channels (App NPS: 20, Web NPS: 23) and among the consumer segment (NPS: 21).

Key driver analysis shows Network Reliability (28.5% relative importance) and Billing Clarity (21.0%) as the two most important drivers of overall satisfaction, yet both score in the bottom quartile of performance (58.2% and 52.4% top-2-box respectively).

Customer churn has increased from 15.8% (2024) to 18.2% (2025), with the highest churn in the consumer segment (21.4%) and among customers in rural areas (23.1%).

The contact centre handled 3.8 million interactions in 2025, up 15% from 2024. Billing-related queries accounted for 28% of all contacts, network complaints 22%, and app-related issues 12%.

3. RESEARCH OBJECTIVES

This advisory engagement aims to:

a) Diagnose the root causes of NPS decline across channels, segments, and regions
b) Prioritise improvement actions using the QTM 8-factor prioritisation framework
c) Quantify the financial impact of recommended improvements
d) Produce stakeholder-appropriate outputs for C-Suite, VP, and operational audiences
e) Establish a baseline for tracking improvement over the next 4 quarters

5. KEY HYPOTHESES

Based on preliminary data review, we hypothesise:

H1: Network reliability in rural areas is the primary driver of NPS decline among the consumer segment
H2: Billing complexity is creating unnecessary contact centre volume and driving detractor creation
H3: The app experience is undermining the digital-first strategy and creating a negative feedback loop (app failure → contact centre call → higher cost-to-serve)
H4: The premium segment represents an underexploited growth opportunity with significantly better unit economics
H5: Agent quality is a competitive strength that is being undermined by product/network issues agents cannot control
