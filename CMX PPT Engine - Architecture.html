CMX Workshop Deck Engine — Proposed Architecture

How pre-workshop survey results and analysis outcomes get integrated into each workshop's PowerPoint deck. This document shows the data flow, slide map, and per-workshop content structure for your review before we build.

Section 1

End-to-End Data Pipeline

Survey responses flow through four stages before becoming slides. Each stage adds intelligence — the deck is never a static template.
1 · Collect

Pre-Workshop Questionnaire

  • Role-adaptive survey (33 Qs for WS1)
  • Branches by Executive / Ops / Tech / Regional
  • Exports as Qualtrics CSV
  • 12+ respondents per workshop
  • Open-ended fields (Q31–Q33)
›
2 · Analyze

Intelligence Engine

  • Role-weighted consensus (1.5× for domain owners)
  • 6 pairwise role divergence scores
  • ADKAR stage mapping per decision
  • 6 risk categories: Sponsorship, Fatigue, Capacity, Data, Alignment, Adoption
  • Open point extraction + auto-categorization
  • Facilitator playbooks per decision
  • Cascade impact analysis
›
3 · Generate

PPT Deck Engine

  • Slide master templates per workshop type
  • Data-bound chart generation (bar, radar, heatmap)
  • Conditional formatting (consensus ≥75% green, 50–75% amber, <50% red)
  • Per-decision slide with full intelligence
  • Risk dashboard with severity badges
  • Stakeholder verbatim quotes injected
  • Facilitator notes in speaker notes
›
4 · Deliver

Workshop-Ready Deck

  • AKT branded, client-specific
  • 25–30 slides per workshop
  • Pre-populated discussion points
  • Facilitator can add/remove slides
  • Speaker notes with talking points
  • Matching XLSX tracker + HTML briefing

Section 5

Design Principles

Three rules that govern how data becomes slides.

Intelligence, Not Information

Never dump raw data. Every number on a slide has a "so what" — a recommendation, a risk signal, or a comparison that tells the facilitator exactly where to focus.

Cascade Continuity

Every workshop deck opens with a recap of prior decisions. Nothing starts from zero. The stakeholders always see how today's session connects to what came before and what comes next.

Facilitator-First

Speaker notes contain the real intelligence: warm-up exercises, watch-outs, alignment techniques. The visible slide is for stakeholders; the notes are for the consultant running the session.
