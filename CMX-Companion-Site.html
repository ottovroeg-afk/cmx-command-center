QTM — Qualtrics Translator Machine

Contextual Insight Engine

A browser-based intelligence engine that takes raw Qualtrics exports (survey, TextIQ, StatsIQ, dashboard, tickets) and strategic context documents (annual reports, C-suite interviews, benchmarks), cross-references them, and produces prioritised advisory recommendations with three AKT-branded PPTX decks tailored to different audiences: C-Level Brief, Operational Playbook, and Webinar.

🧠 How QTM Builds Intelligence

The engine classifies uploads into ten analytical buckets (KPI metrics, trends, target gaps, drivers, tickets, verbatims, benchmarks, stakeholder interviews, strategy context, financial data), then triangulates signals across sources to build confidence-scored priority pockets.

Each finding is scored across eight weighted factors: Customer Impact (20%), Business Impact (18%), Urgency (15%), Strategic Importance (12%), Confidence (10%), Recurrence (10%), Stakeholder Relevance (8%), and Effort (7%).

Zero AI dependency at runtime. All logic is deterministic and rule-based. Every recommendation traces back to a source file and row. No hallucination, no black box, no variable output quality. 30 seconds from upload to insight.

CMX Command Center

Program Mission Control

A connected automation system that transforms how AKT prepares for and delivers CMX workshops. Collects structured stakeholder input via role-adaptive questionnaires, auto-analyses consensus, divergence, risk signals, and ADKAR readiness across all 8 governance decisions, then generates pre-populated PPTX decks, XLSX trackers, and HTML facilitator briefings — all seeded with real stakeholder data. One consultant, full program visibility across 6 workshop stages.

🧠 How the Intelligence Engine Works

The engine produces consultant-grade analysis, not just data counts. Role-weighted consensus: executive answers weighted 1.5× on governance decisions, technical answers weighted 1.5× on data decisions — reflecting real influence dynamics.

ADKAR stage mapping identifies where the change journey is weakest. Divergence detection surfaces hidden tensions between role pairs (Executive vs Regional, Technical vs Operational). Six CMX-specific risk categories: Sponsorship, Fatigue, Capacity, Data, Alignment, Adoption.

Cascade intelligence: each workshop's outputs automatically seed the next workshop's inputs. WS1 governance decisions feed into WS2 stakeholder mapping, WS2 shapes survey design, WS3 guides platform configuration, WS4 enables decision workshops. Intelligence compounds across the program.
